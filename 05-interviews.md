# Interview Documentation

## Interview 1: Hospital Administrator
- **Date**: June 15, 2026
- **Duration**: 30 minutes
- **Method**: In-person

### Questions Asked
1. What are your biggest challenges in blood management?
2. How do you currently track blood inventory?
3. What features would help you most?
4. What concerns do you have about a digital system?

### Key Responses
1. "We often run out of certain blood types during emergencies"
2. "We use manual records and spreadsheets currently"
3. "Real-time tracking and quick alerts would be game-changing"
4. "Data security and reliability are my main concerns"

### Insights Gained
- Manual tracking is error-prone
- Urgent needs require immediate attention
- Staff needs training on new systems
- Integration with existing systems needed

## Interview 2: Regular Donor
- **Date**: June 16, 2026
- **Duration**: 20 minutes
- **Method**: Phone

### Questions Asked
1. What motivates you to donate blood?
2. What are your frustrations with current process?
3. What would make donating easier?
4. How do you prefer to be contacted?

### Key Responses
1. "I want to help save lives"
2. "The process takes too long"
3. "Online booking and reminders would be great"
4. "SMS or WhatsApp notifications"

### Insights Gained
- Convenience is key
- Donors want recognition
- Communication is important
- Health updates matter

## Interview 3: Recipient
- **Date**: June 16, 2026
- **Duration**: 25 minutes
- **Method**: Video call

### Questions Asked
1. How did you find blood when you needed it?
2. What was your experience like?
3. What would have made it easier?
4. Any suggestions for the system?

### Key Responses
1. "It was very stressful and time-consuming"
2. "The process was not transparent"
3. "Real-time availability would have been helpful"
4. "Should be available 24/7"

### Insights Gained
- Transparency is critical
- Quick response needed
- User-friendly interface required
- Emergency protocols important
